# Living Landscape: Unofficial Atiwa Fan Expansion

> **Baseline experiment:** This v0.1 scoring-only design is preserved for
> comparison. The active design experiment is the public, declining-reward
> Open Commitment race in `docs/atiwa_open_commitments_v0_3.md`.

## Design Contract

- **Status:** Design complete for text-only prototype; not yet playtested
- **Version:** 0.1
- **Base game required:** *Atiwa* (Uwe Rosenberg, Lookout Games, 2022)
- **Players:** 1-4
- **Modules:** Landscape Priorities and Stewardship Commitments

This document is the authoritative design contract for the first prototype of
*Living Landscape*. It contains the complete rules and card text for both
modules, the reference sheet, and the revised scoring sheet. Graphic design and
automated modeling must follow this contract until playtesting produces a
recorded revision.

The expansion is intended as free, noncommercial, unofficial fan content. It
must not reproduce Lookout Games artwork, logos, card layouts, or rulebook
graphics. Public release remains blocked on an offline review of the
BoardGameGeek variants and files sections, a licensing and attribution review,
and the playtest gates listed below.

## 1. Design Problem

Atiwa's central ecosystem is satisfying, but repeated games can converge on the
same broad plan:

- clear the most valuable rows of the supply board;
- train families to avoid pollution and earn stable income;
- expand the tableau enough to hold the resulting resources;
- score the normal mixture of cards, families, gold, and fruit bats.

The base game already varies the terrain display and the order in which action
space tiles become available. That variability changes tactics and timing more
than it changes what a successful tableau is trying to become. The expansion
therefore changes scoring incentives rather than adding another production
system.

## 2. Design Goals

The expansion must:

1. Create materially different reasons to value Terrain cards, Location cards,
   token placement, tableau geometry, and individual resource types.
2. Preserve the base game's seven rounds, three workers, worker-placement
   tension, maintenance sequence, pollution rules, and fruit bat action.
3. Use only cards and a scoring sheet. It must not require new wooden pieces,
   tracks, currencies, or markers.
4. Keep every new effect at end-game scoring in version 0.1. No card grants an
   action, discount, income, conversion, or persistent power.
5. Make the shared strategic environment vary from game to game while also
   giving individual players different incentives.
6. Remain usable as either one module or as the recommended combined expansion.
7. Add no upkeep during rounds I-III and only one simultaneous decision at the
   beginning of round IV.
8. Preserve Atiwa's environmental message. Mining and pollution must not become
   a profitable engine that can be erased or repaired without consequence.
9. Work at every base-game player count, including solo.

## 3. Non-Goals

Version 0.1 does not:

- add new Terrain or Location cards to the base decks;
- add or replace action spaces or action space tiles;
- change feeding, breeding, income, training, or pollution;
- create asymmetric player powers;
- add events, disasters, take-that effects, or direct resource theft;
- rebalance the base game's printed cards or supply boards;
- replace the base game's solo worker-blocking system;
- claim to be an official expansion or use official graphic assets;
- attempt to teach ecological claims through unsourced flavor text.

Those ideas may be considered only after the scoring modules have been tested
on their own.

## 4. Components

The complete expansion contains:

- 8 Landscape Priority cards, IDs `LP01` through `LP08`;
- 12 Stewardship Commitment cards, IDs `SC01` through `SC12`;
- 1 double-sided reference sheet or one reference card per player;
- 1 revised scoring sheet;
- this rules and design document.

No expansion component is shuffled into a base-game deck.

## 5. Module Overview

### Module 1: Landscape Priorities

Landscape Priorities are public scoring conditions shared by all players.
Reveal two at setup. Each player scores both at the end of the game.

They primarily reward tableau structure, relationships between cards, and the
placement of resources on particular cards. Because every player sees the same
priorities, they also increase competition for relevant base-game action
spaces and face-up Terrain cards.

### Module 2: Stewardship Commitments

Stewardship Commitments are personal scoring conditions. Each player receives
two possibilities at setup, chooses one at the beginning of round IV, and
reveals that choice to all players.

They primarily reward specializing in a resource, community pattern, or form
of stewardship. The delayed choice lets players respond to the Terrain cards
and opportunities seen during the first three rounds. Public commitment in
round IV gives opponents time to understand and contest the chosen plan.

### Recommended Game

Use both modules together. Module 1 supplies a changing shared puzzle; Module 2
creates divergent player incentives inside that puzzle.

## 6. Complete Rules

### 6.1 Setup

Set up Atiwa normally. Then apply the setup steps for each module being used.

**Landscape Priorities**

1. Shuffle the 8 Landscape Priority cards.
2. Reveal 2 cards face up beside the action board.
3. Return the other 6 cards to the box without looking at them.

Both revealed cards remain in effect for the entire game. All players may
inspect them at any time.

**Stewardship Commitments**

1. Shuffle the 12 Stewardship Commitment cards.
2. Deal 2 cards face down to each player.
3. Each player looks at their own 2 cards and keeps both secret.
4. Return undealt cards to the box without looking at them.

The initial two-card deal is not a draft. A player may plan toward either or
both cards during the first three rounds but will eventually score only one.

### 6.2 Rounds I-III

Play Atiwa normally. There are no additional phases, actions, or checks.

Players may look at their own Stewardship Commitment cards at any time. Until
round IV, they may not show a card, name it, read its text aloud, or otherwise
confirm its exact identity. They may still discuss their tableau and general
plans normally.

### 6.3 Commitment at the Beginning of Round IV

After completing the maintenance phase of round III but before the starting
player takes the first turn of round IV's work phase:

1. Each player secretly chooses 1 of their 2 Stewardship Commitment cards.
2. All players reveal their chosen card simultaneously.
3. Place the chosen card face up beside that player's tableau.
4. Return the unchosen card face down to the box.

The choice is final. A revealed Commitment cannot be exchanged, copied, or
discarded.

If a player forgets to choose, stop at the first opportunity before another
worker is placed, make the choice, and continue. Do not rewind completed
actions. If a player reaches final scoring without revealing a Commitment,
they score 0 points for Module 2.

### 6.4 Rounds IV-VII

Play Atiwa normally. Revealed Commitments provide no ability during play; they
only describe end-game scoring.

### 6.5 Game End

The game still ends at the normal time: after step 5, Breeding, in the
maintenance phase of round VII.

Score in this order:

1. Score every normal Atiwa category.
2. Record the **Base Game Subtotal**.
3. Score the first revealed Landscape Priority.
4. Score the second revealed Landscape Priority.
5. Score the player's revealed Stewardship Commitment.
6. Add those three expansion rows as the **Living Landscape Subtotal**.
7. Add both subtotals to obtain the **Final Total**.

When using only one module, enter 0 in the unused module's scoring row or rows.

The player with the highest Final Total wins. Resolve a tie using Atiwa's
normal tie-breaker: fewer pollution markers. If still tied, share the victory.

Expansion cards never score negative points. Each printed maximum applies
after all parts of that card have been calculated.

## 7. Rules Vocabulary

The following definitions apply to every expansion component.

### Card

In scoring text, a **card** is a base-game Terrain or Location card in a
player's tableau. It is not an expansion card, overview card, or Night card.

### Contains

A card **contains** a token if that token is physically placed on that card.

- Fruit placed on a tree is contained by the card holding that tree.
- A fruit bat placed on a trained family is contained by the card holding that
  family.
- Tokens on the Night card, supply board, action board, or in the general
  supply are not contained by a tableau card.

### Living Token Type

There are six **living token types** for this expansion:

1. wild animal;
2. tree;
3. fruit;
4. fruit bat;
5. family, trained or untrained;
6. goat.

Gold, workers, pollution markers, and printed icons are not living token
types. A tree with fruit contributes two living token types: tree and fruit. A
trained family holding a fruit bat contributes family and fruit bat.

### Fruit-Bearing Tree

A **fruit-bearing tree** is a tree token with a fruit token physically placed
on it. A tree and a separate fruit elsewhere on the same card do not form a
fruit-bearing tree for expansion scoring.

### Trained Family Housing a Fruit Bat

A **trained family housing a fruit bat** is a trained family token with a fruit
bat token physically placed on that family. A trained family and a separate
fruit bat elsewhere on the same card do not satisfy this phrase.

### Unpolluted Card

An **unpolluted card** has no pollution markers anywhere on it. A card ceases
to be unpolluted as soon as it receives its first pollution marker. Empty
blocked or printed spaces do not count as pollution.

### Orthogonally Adjacent

Two tableau cards are **orthogonally adjacent** when they share a complete
horizontal or vertical edge. Diagonal contact does not count.

### Connected Group

A **connected group** is a set of qualifying cards linked through one or more
orthogonal adjacencies. Every card in the path between two cards must itself
meet the scoring condition. A nonqualifying card breaks the group.

### Pair or Set

When a card asks a player to form pairs or sets, each physical card or token
may be assigned to only one of those pairs or sets unless the scoring text
explicitly says otherwise. The player may choose the assignment that produces
the highest legal score.

### Different Card or Different Location Type

"Different card" means that no single tableau card can be counted twice for
that instruction. The four Location types are Farmstead, Settlement, Village,
and Town. Multiple cards of the same Location type still represent only one
type.

### Nature Icon

A **nature icon** is the leaf/nature icon printed on a Terrain card and used by
base-game actions that count nature icons. Token artwork and decorative leaves
do not count.

## 8. Module 1: Complete Landscape Priority Card Text

All Landscape Priority cards use the following footer:

> Score at the end of the game. This card provides no ability during play.

### LP01 - Canopy Corridor

*Connect restored tree cover across the landscape.*

> Find your largest connected group of Terrain cards in which every card
> contains at least 1 tree. Score **2 VP per card** in that group, to a maximum
> of **10 VP**.

### LP02 - Mixed Landscape

*Keep community development and working land connected.*

> Form pairs of orthogonally adjacent cards. Each pair must contain 1 Terrain
> card and 1 Location card. Each card can belong to only 1 pair. Score **2 VP
> per pair**, to a maximum of **10 VP**.

### LP03 - Biodiversity Plots

*Support several forms of life on the same land.*

> Score **2 VP per Terrain card** that contains at least **3 different living
> token types**, to a maximum of **10 VP**.

### LP04 - Seed Dispersal Network

*Build places where fruit trees and fruit bats thrive together.*

> Score **2 VP per Terrain card** that contains both a **fruit-bearing tree**
> and at least **1 fruit bat**, to a maximum of **10 VP**. The fruit bat does
> not need to be on the tree.

### LP05 - Community Education

*Link trained families with safe places for fruit bats.*

> Score **2 VP per Location card** that contains both a **trained family** and
> at least **1 fruit bat**, to a maximum of **10 VP**. The fruit bat does not
> need to be on that family.

### LP06 - Protected Watershed

*Keep productive habitat free from mining pollution.*

> Score **2 VP per unpolluted Terrain card** that contains at least **1 tree or
> 1 wild animal**, to a maximum of **10 VP**.

### LP07 - Connected Communities

*Develop neighboring communities rather than isolated settlements.*

> Find your largest connected group of Location cards in which every card
> contains at least **1 family**. Score **2 VP per card** in that group, to a
> maximum of **10 VP**.

### LP08 - Habitat Edges

*Place wildlife habitat beside fruiting forest.*

> Score **2 VP per Terrain card containing a wild animal** that is
> orthogonally adjacent to at least 1 other Terrain card containing a
> **fruit-bearing tree**, to a maximum of **10 VP**. Count each wild-animal
> Terrain at most once, even if it touches several qualifying cards. A single
> fruit-bearing Terrain may support more than one adjacent wild-animal
> Terrain.

## 9. Module 2: Complete Stewardship Commitment Card Text

All Stewardship Commitment cards use the following footer:

> Reveal at the beginning of round IV. Score at the end of the game. This card
> provides no ability during play.

### SC01 - Wildlife Steward

*Maintain wildlife across several habitats.*

> Score **2 VP per different Terrain card** containing at least **1 wild
> animal**, to a maximum of **12 VP**.

### SC02 - Forest Restorer

*Return tree cover to working land.*

> Score **1 VP per tree on Terrain cards**, to a maximum of **12 VP**. Trees on
> Location cards do not count. A fruit-bearing tree is still one tree.

### SC03 - Orchard Grower

*Establish fruiting trees across several plots.*

> Score **2 VP per different Terrain card** containing at least **1
> fruit-bearing tree**, to a maximum of **12 VP**.

### SC04 - Bat Guardian

*Make trained households safe places for fruit bats.*

> Score **2 VP per trained family housing a fruit bat**, to a maximum of
> **12 VP**.

### SC05 - Food Security Cooperative

*Balance the needs of families with dependable livestock.*

> Form sets containing **1 family and 1 goat** from anywhere in your tableau.
> Each token can belong to only 1 set. Score **2 VP per set**, to a maximum of
> **12 VP**. Trained and untrained families both count.

### SC06 - Community Educator

*Share conservation knowledge across many communities.*

> Score **2 VP per different Location card** containing at least **1 trained
> family**, to a maximum of **12 VP**. Multiple trained families on the same
> Location still score only 2 VP.

### SC07 - Settlement Network

*Support families in communities of different sizes.*

> Score **3 VP per different Location type** in which you have at least 1 card
> containing a family, to a maximum of **12 VP**. The Location types are
> Farmstead, Settlement, Village, and Town. Trained and untrained families both
> count.

### SC08 - Forest Advocate

*Protect land with high natural value.*

> Score **2 VP per Terrain card showing a nature icon**, to a maximum of
> **12 VP**.

### SC09 - Low-Impact Prosperity

*Build lasting income without accepting widespread pollution.*

> Score **1 VP per 2 gold** you have, rounding down, to a maximum of **8 VP
> from gold**. Then score **4 additional VP** if your entire tableau contains
> no more than **1 pollution marker**. This card provides at most **12 VP**.

### SC10 - Land Steward

*Keep trees and wildlife together on undamaged land.*

> Score **2 VP per unpolluted Terrain card** containing both at least **1 tree**
> and at least **1 wild animal**, to a maximum of **12 VP**.

### SC11 - Balanced Ecology

*Maintain a broad base of food, habitat, and livestock.*

> Form sets containing **1 wild animal, 1 tree, 1 fruit, and 1 goat** from
> anywhere in your tableau. Each token can belong to only 1 set. Score **2 VP
> per set**, to a maximum of **12 VP**. Fruit on a tree and that tree are two
> separate tokens and may be used in the same set.

### SC12 - Fruit Bat Census

*Provide room for a large and visible fruit bat population.*

> Score **1 VP per 2 fruit bats in your tableau**, rounding down, to a maximum
> of **12 VP**. Count fruit bats on trained families. Do not count fruit bats
> outside your tableau. This is in addition to normal Atiwa fruit bat scoring.

## 10. Scoring Examples and Edge Cases

These examples are rules, not optional guidance.

### Canopy Corridor

A player has two separate connected groups of qualifying tree-bearing Terrain
cards, one containing 3 cards and one containing 5. Only the larger group
scores: `5 x 2 = 10 VP`.

### Mixed Landscape

A Location touches two Terrain cards. It can pair with either one, not both. A
player with six legal adjacencies but only four disjoint pairs scores 8 VP.

### Biodiversity Plots

A Terrain containing a trained family with a fruit bat and a fruit-bearing
tree contains four living token types: family, fruit bat, tree, and fruit. It
qualifies even if it contains no wild animal or goat.

### Seed Dispersal Network

A Terrain with a tree, a separate fruit, and a fruit bat does not qualify. The
fruit must be physically on a tree. The fruit bat may be anywhere legal on that
Terrain.

### Community Education

A Location with a trained family and a fruit bat on a different legal space of
that Location qualifies. Unlike Bat Guardian, the fruit bat need not be on the
family.

### Habitat Edges

One Terrain containing a fruit-bearing tree is adjacent to three Terrain cards
that each contain a wild animal. All three wild-animal Terrain cards qualify
and score 6 VP total. The central fruit-bearing Terrain does not itself score
unless it also contains a wild animal and is adjacent to a different
fruit-bearing Terrain.

### Food Security Cooperative

A player has 7 families and 4 goats. They can form four sets and score 8 VP.
The current food demand and whether the families are trained do not affect this
card.

### Low-Impact Prosperity

A player with 19 gold and one pollution marker scores 9 VP from gold, but the
gold portion is capped at 8, then scores the 4-point clean-development bonus,
for 12 VP total. A player with 19 gold and two pollution markers scores only
8 VP.

### Balanced Ecology

A player has 5 wild animals, 8 trees, 3 fruit, and 4 goats. They can form three
sets and score 6 VP. Token position and whether fruit is on a tree do not
otherwise matter.

### Fruit Bat Census

A player with 19 fruit bats in their tableau scores 9 VP from this Commitment.
They also score the base game's normal fruit bat points separately.

## 11. Complete Reference Sheet Text

The printable reference must contain the following text. It may be reformatted
or split across two sides, but its wording and rules content must not change
without updating this design contract.

---

### LIVING LANDSCAPE - REFERENCE

#### Setup

**Landscape Priorities:** Shuffle 8. Reveal 2 beside the action board. Every
player scores both at game end.

**Stewardship Commitments:** Deal 2 face down to each player. Look at your own
cards and keep them secret. Return unused cards to the box.

#### Beginning of Round IV

Before the first worker is placed, each player secretly chooses 1 Commitment.
Reveal all choices simultaneously. Keep the chosen card face up and return the
other face down to the box. The choice is final.

#### During Play

Expansion cards grant no actions, income, discounts, conversions, or other
abilities. Play Atiwa normally.

#### Game End

After normal round VII Breeding:

1. score all normal Atiwa categories;
2. record the Base Game Subtotal;
3. score both Landscape Priorities;
4. score your revealed Stewardship Commitment;
5. add the Living Landscape Subtotal to the Base Game Subtotal.

Highest Final Total wins. Break ties normally: fewer pollution markers, then
shared victory.

#### Key Terms

**Contains:** physically placed on that tableau card. Tokens elsewhere do not
count.

**Living token types:** wild animal, tree, fruit, fruit bat, family, and goat.

**Fruit-bearing tree:** a tree with fruit physically on it.

**Housing a fruit bat:** the bat is physically on that trained family.

**Unpolluted card:** a card with no pollution markers.

**Orthogonally adjacent:** sharing a horizontal or vertical edge, not diagonal.

**Connected group:** qualifying cards linked orthogonally through other
qualifying cards.

**Pairs/sets:** each named physical card or token can be used only once unless
the scoring card says otherwise.

**Nature icon:** the printed leaf/nature icon counted by normal Atiwa actions.

All expansion scoring occurs only at game end and cannot be negative. Apply
the maximum printed on each card.

## 13. Solo Rules

Use the base game's normal solo action-board extension, worker colors, worker
blocking, Terrain replenishment, and seven-round structure.

Apply each expansion module being used exactly as in multiplayer:

- if using Module 1, reveal two public Landscape Priorities and score both at
  game end;
- if using Module 2, deal two private Commitments, reveal one at the beginning
  of round IV, and score the revealed Commitment at game end.

To win the prototype solo game, satisfy both conditions:

1. score at least 120 base-game points before expansion scoring; and
2. meet the expansion threshold for the module or modules used:
   - Landscape Priorities only: 8 points;
   - Stewardship Commitment only: 6 points;
   - both modules: 14 points.

This dual threshold prevents expansion bonuses from making the official solo
target easier. All three expansion requirements are tuning values, not yet
validated difficulty levels.

## 14. PnP Production Specification

### Card Format

- Use 63 x 88 mm portrait cards or another standard sleeved size.
- Landscape Priority and Stewardship Commitment backs must be visibly
  different.
- Each face must show its card ID, module name, title, complete scoring text,
  maximum, and the standard module footer.
- Card IDs must remain visible after sleeving.
- The cards do not need to resemble Atiwa's square cards because they are
  never mixed into a base-game deck.

### Accessibility

- Every icon must also have a text label or be explained on the same card.
- No rule may depend on color alone.
- The release must include a high-contrast, low-ink version.
- Body text should be at least 9 points at final printed size where practical.
- Maximum scores should be typographically prominent.

### Proposed Page Count

- three card sheets for 20 cards;
- one rules/reference sheet, preferably double-sided;
- one scoring sheet that may be printed repeatedly.

### Art and Branding

- Use original, commissioned, public-domain, or appropriately licensed art.
- Do not trace or reproduce base-game card art, icons, logos, fonts, textures,
  or layouts.
- The cover should say: **Living Landscape - an unofficial fan expansion
  compatible with Atiwa. Base game required.**
- Credit Uwe Rosenberg as the designer of Atiwa and Lookout Games as its
  publisher, without implying endorsement.
- Include a clear noncommercial fan-content notice.
- Select and record a license for the expansion's original text and art before
  public release. The license decision is not made by this design document.

## 15. Ecological and Cultural Treatment

The game title uses *Atiwa*. Conservation organizations and research sources
commonly use *Atewa* for the real forest range. Release copy must preserve that
distinction rather than silently treating the spellings as interchangeable.

The expansion's themes are grounded in documented work involving habitat
restoration, conservation education, stakeholder dialogue, community-led
governance, agroforestry, biodiversity, and protection of the forest's water
functions. Card faces deliberately use short thematic prompts rather than
specific factual claims.

Before release:

1. ask a Ghanaian cultural or conservation reviewer to review terminology,
   framing, and art direction;
2. avoid fictional named Ghanaian characters unless developed with appropriate
   consultation;
3. include a short source and further-reading section;
4. consider highlighting or supporting a Ghanaian-led conservation
   organization rather than presenting the expansion itself as advocacy on
   their behalf.

Starting research references:

- [Official Atiwa rules](https://www.lookout-spiele.de/upload/en_atiwa.html_Atiwa_Rules_161_EN_WEB_compressed.pdf)
- [Official accompanying booklet](https://lookout-spiele.de/upload/en_atiwa.html_Atiwa_Begleitheft_EN_WEB.pdf)
- [Save Atewa Forest](https://www.atewa.org/index.php/about-us/)
- [Rufford Foundation: fruit bat conservation in Atewa](https://www.rufford.org/projects/ebenezer-kofi-badu/survey-and-conservation-of-pohles-fruit-bat-in-the-eastern-region-of-ghana/)
- [Community-led Atewa landscape governance project](https://www.programmeppi.org/en/projects/community-led-governance-and-management-of-atewa-forest-range-landscape/)

## 16. Balance Model

### Point Budget

- Each Landscape Priority has a maximum of 10 VP.
- Each Stewardship Commitment has a maximum of 12 VP.
- The absolute expansion maximum is 32 VP.
- The initial target is an average Living Landscape Subtotal of 16-24 VP for a
  player who makes meaningful strategic concessions.
- A player who ignores all three cards should generally score 5-12 points from
  incidental overlap, not zero and not a competitive maximum.

The expansion is intentionally large enough to change decisions but not large
enough to make normal Atiwa scoring irrelevant.

### Balance Principles

1. A 10- or 12-point score must normally require several worker placements,
   placement concessions, or contested cards beyond an ordinary baseline
   game.
2. No card should reward pollution itself, erase pollution, or provide points
   for untrained families merely because they create pollution.
3. Shared Priorities may overlap with a Commitment. Such synergy is desirable,
   but no pairing should produce its combined maximum without substantial
   commitment.
4. Spatial cards should be checked for Terrain-draw dependence. The two-card
   Commitment choice mitigates but does not eliminate draw luck.
5. Resource-count cards should be checked against the base supply-board point
   curve so that doubling an already efficient route does not dominate.
6. Since scoring cards grant no in-game benefits, they cannot directly create
   a runaway economic leader.
7. Public objectives must not include first-to-achieve races in version 0.1;
   that would amplify turn order and create additional timing rules.

## 17. Playtest Plan

### Stage 1: Legality and Comprehension

Run open-information test games with all card text visible. Confirm:

- every condition can be evaluated from the final tableau;
- no condition conflicts with base placement rules;
- different players independently calculate the same score;
- all reference-sheet definitions answer common questions;
- choosing at the beginning of round IV is memorable and properly timed.

### Stage 2: Card Feasibility

Test every Landscape Priority at least five times and every Commitment at least
five times across recorded games. For each card record:

- player count;
- points scored;
- whether the player deliberately pursued it;
- estimated worker placements or card-placement concessions made for it;
- incidental score when not pursued;
- confusing interactions;
- whether a public and private pairing produced excessive synergy.

### Stage 3: Player-Count Coverage

Before release, complete at minimum:

- 10 solo games;
- 10 two-player games;
- 6 three-player games;
- 6 four-player games.

These may overlap Stage 2. Human games are required; automated modeling may
supplement but not replace them.

### Stage 4: Blind Rules Test

Give the text-only package to players who know Atiwa but have not been taught
Living Landscape. They must be able to set it up, commit in round IV, and score
without designer intervention.

## 19. Fixed Decisions and Tuning Knobs

The following are fixed for the first text-only prototype:

- exactly two public Priorities;
- exactly two private Commitments dealt to each player;
- one Commitment revealed at the beginning of round IV;
- end-game scoring only;
- no new actions, components, currencies, or powers;
- individual card caps of 10 VP for Priorities and 12 VP for Commitments;
- expansion score kept separate from the base subtotal in solo play.

The following may be tuned after recorded tests:

- individual multipliers, thresholds, and caps;
- the 8-, 6-, and 14-point solo expansion requirements;
- individual card wording where clarification does not change intent;
- the precise card size, page imposition, and visual design;
- replacement of a card whose core condition proves unworkable.

Changing module timing, adding powers, adding a third public Priority, or
changing the number of private cards dealt is a design revision, not routine
balance tuning. Such a change requires a new contract version and renewed
full-set testing.

## 20. Deferred Ideas

The following are explicitly parked until after version 0.1:

- asymmetric community-role powers;
- new action space tiles;
- shared project races or first-player rewards;
- annual weather or policy events;
- restoration of polluted spaces;
- new Terrain and Location cards;
- campaign progression;
- achievements derived from the official solo challenges;
- named historical or contemporary figures;
- additional resource types.

The official solo challenges already explore Colony, Commonwealth, Investor,
Metropolis, Migration, No Pollution, Prosperity, and Untrained play. Future
content should not merely reproduce those challenges as multiplayer cards.

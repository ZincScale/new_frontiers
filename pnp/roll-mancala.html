Mancala Rules + Solo Aid

Stones stay on the board

Round Sequence

  1. Choose a board section or one Spent color group.
  2. Reveal choices and sow.
  3. The final landing section selects your phase.
  4. Resolve selected phases in Roll order.
  5. Manage Empire; you may buy recovery sows.

No Roll Dice step. No Assign Dice step. VP pool: 8/player.

Sow From Board

  1. Pick up all dice from one section.
  2. Choose their order.
  3. Sow clockwise, one die per section.
  4. Skip full sections.
  5. Final landing section selects your phase.

Loop: Explore -> Develop -> Settle -> Produce -> Ship.

Core Rule

  • Dice are mancala stones, not workers.
  • Stones stay in sections when phases resolve.
  • Develop/Settle are paid with Credits.
  • Goods are markers on Worlds, not dice.
  • Spent is off-board recovery and overflow.

Match Bonuses

Blue in Explore
+1 Explore action.
Brown in Develop
-1 Credit cost this Develop.
Red in Settle
-1 Credit cost this Settle.
Green in Produce
+1 Produce action.
Purple in Ship
+1 VP on first Consume.

Check only the final die of each sow.

Gain / Lose

  • Gain a die: add it to its matching section.
  • White and yellow gains go to Spent.
  • If matching section is full, gain to Spent.
  • Lose a die: remove that physical die.
  • Capacity is the dice you own.

Builds

  • Keep one Development and one World under construction.
  • Pay Credits equal cost minus section discount.
  • Develop discounts: Brown, White, Yellow in Develop.
  • Settle discounts: Red, White, Yellow in Settle.
  • Match bonus reduces the same build by 1 more.
  • No partial progress is kept.

Phase Actions

  • Explore: fill empty queues, then gain 2 Credits.
  • Produce: place Good markers on eligible Worlds.
  • Ship: trade Goods if Credits are low, otherwise Consume.
  • Credits never become dice.
  • Manage Empire: spend 2 Credits for a recovery sow.

Solo Summary

  • Use one player setup and 30 VP chips.
  • Shuffle 5 Dummy phase cards.
  • Each round, draw 2 Dummy cards.
  • Play until 12 tableau cards, empty VP pool, or round 16.
  • Owned dice means sections plus Spent.

Tile Text

  • Use normal Roll meaning first.
  • Translate dice into mancala stones.
  • No Dice Cup, Citizenry, Wild phase, or Alien phase.
  • Sections set phase strength and discounts.
  • Color remains identity for tile text and scoring.

Flex Dice

  • Gain Alien Technology die: yellow to Spent.
  • Gain white die: white to Spent.
  • When recovered, choose any normal phase entry section.
  • Yellow and white work only in their current section's phase.
  • Neither color has its own mancala phase.

Lose Dice

  • Lose Military die: remove one red die.
  • Lose Novelty die: remove one blue die.
  • Lose any die: choose one physical die owned.
  • Default priority: Spent, then a board section.
  • If you do not own that color, ignore the loss.

Temporary Help

  • Extra phase worker becomes one temporary action.
  • Extra Develop/Settle worker becomes -1 Credit cost.
  • Cost reduction reduces matching Credit cost.
  • Place-a-die effects create Good markers when producing.
  • Ignore lasting worker-progress placement.

Costs / Credits

  • Cost reductions apply when completing a tile.
  • Develop -1 reduces one Development Credit cost.
  • Settle -1 reduces one World Credit cost.
  • Credits recover Spent dice during Manage Empire.
  • Credits also pay Develop and Settle costs.
  • Credits are never stones.

Produce / Ship

  • Produce places a Good marker on an eligible World.
  • The Good marker is not a die and has no color.
  • Ship consumes or trades Good markers.
  • Ship stones remain on the board.
  • Remove shipped Good markers from the World.

Alien / White

  • Alien means yellow identity, tag, or world type.
  • Alien never means a separate phase section.
  • Yellow in Settle can settle; yellow in Ship can ship.
  • White is flexible but still section-bound.
  • White/yellow do not trigger section match bonuses.

Dice Scoring

  • Specific-color dice bonuses count physical dice in sections and Spent.
  • Military dice means red dice. Alien Technology dice means yellow dice.
  • Good markers and queued tiles never count as owned dice.
